# 📈 Community Bots

> Bots focused on engagement, leveling, reputation systems, member retention, progression, and community growth.

**Navigation:** [Main Directory](../README.md) · [All Categories](../README.md#-categories)

Community bots help transform a Discord server from a simple chat room into an active, engaging community. They encourage participation through leveling systems, achievements, reputation mechanics, profiles, rewards, leaderboards, and social features.

This category covers bots designed to increase activity, reward engagement, improve retention, and create long-term progression systems for members.

## 📌 Category Overview

### What this category covers

Bots in this category provide one or more of the following:

* Leveling systems
* XP progression
* Reputation systems
* User profiles
* Role rewards
* Activity tracking
* Community engagement
* Achievements
* Leaderboards
* Retention mechanics
* Social interaction systems

### What qualifies for inclusion

A bot belongs in this category if it:

* Primarily focuses on community engagement
* Encourages member participation
* Provides progression or reward systems
* Is actively maintained and publicly available

### What does NOT qualify

The following do not belong in this category:

* Moderation-first bots
* Economy-only bots
* Analytics platforms
* Utility bots with basic leveling features
* Abandoned engagement systems

## 🤖 Bots in This Category

### [Arcane](../bots/community/arcane.md)

One of the most popular leveling bots available. Arcane supports both text and voice activity leveling, role rewards, leaderboards, and engagement tracking.

**Best for:** General community growth.
**Free tier:** Generous.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [Lurkr](../bots/community/lurkr.md)

An engagement platform focused on progression systems, achievements, rewards, and community retention mechanics.

**Best for:** Gamified communities.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [MEE6](../bots/community/mee6.md)

A widely recognized all-in-one community management bot featuring leveling, role rewards, announcements, and engagement systems.

**Best for:** Beginner-friendly community management.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [Tatsu](../bots/community/tatsu.md)

A long-running community bot featuring profiles, reputation systems, collectibles, leveling, and social interaction mechanics.

**Best for:** Social and profile-driven communities.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [Streamcord](../bots/community/streamcord.md)

A community-focused bot that helps creators notify members about Twitch and streaming activity while supporting community engagement.

**Best for:** Creator communities.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

## 🎯 Choosing the Right Bot

| If your priority is...     | Consider   |
| -------------------------- | ---------- |
| Best leveling system       | Arcane     |
| Rich member profiles       | Tatsu      |
| Gamification               | Lurkr      |
| Creator growth             | Streamcord |
| Easy setup                 | MEE6       |
| Large community engagement | Arcane     |

### Key Questions

**Do you want members to compete through XP?**
→ Arcane or MEE6.

**Do you want social features and profiles?**
→ Tatsu.

**Do you want achievement-based progression?**
→ Lurkr.

**Do you run a creator-focused server?**
→ Streamcord.

## 📝 Contributor Notes

### Adding a Community Bot

* Engagement must be the bot's primary purpose.
* Verify leveling and progression features.
* Confirm active maintenance.

### Category-Specific Notes

* Many bots mix moderation and engagement features.
* Only include bots where community engagement is a major focus.
* Re-evaluate premium restrictions periodically.
