# 🎫 Ticket Bots

> Bots focused on support systems, applications, customer service, staff communication, and structured request management.

**Navigation:** [Main Directory](../README.md) · [All Categories](../README.md#-categories)

Ticket bots help communities organize support requests, staff applications, bug reports, appeals, partnership requests, and other structured interactions. Instead of handling requests in public channels, ticket systems create dedicated spaces where staff and users can communicate privately.

This category covers ticket management systems, support desks, application workflows, modmail platforms, and customer support solutions.

## 📌 Category Overview

### What this category covers

Bots in this category provide one or more of the following:

* Support ticket systems
* Staff applications
* Partnership applications
* Bug reporting systems
* Appeal systems
* Ticket transcripts
* Form submissions
* Customer support workflows
* Department routing

### What qualifies for inclusion

A bot belongs in this category if it:

* Primarily focuses on tickets or support workflows
* Is actively maintained
* Supports structured communication between users and staff
* Has a public invite and documentation

### What does NOT qualify

The following do not belong in this category:

* General moderation bots
* Form-only bots without ticket functionality
* Utility bots with minor ticket features
* Abandoned support platforms
* Private customer support systems

## 🤖 Bots in This Category

### [Helper.gg](../bots/tickets/helper-gg.md)

A support-focused platform built for communities that handle large volumes of requests. Features include ticket routing, agent assignment, analytics, and support management tools.

**Best for:** Large support teams.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [Modmail](../bots/tickets/modmail.md)

An open-source ticket platform that allows users to contact staff directly through DMs. Conversations are synchronized into private staff channels.

**Best for:** Communities that prefer DM-based support.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Limited.
**Open source:** Yes.

---

### [Ticket Tool](../bots/tickets/ticket-tool.md)

One of Discord's most popular ticket systems. Supports ticket panels, transcripts, permissions, and multiple ticket categories.

**Best for:** General-purpose support.
**Free tier:** Generous.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

---

### [Ticketeer](../bots/tickets/ticketeer.md)

A ticket and application platform focused on collecting structured information before opening support channels.

**Best for:** Applications and onboarding workflows.
**Free tier:** Available.
**Dashboard:** Yes.
**Open source:** No.

## 🎯 Choosing the Right Bot

| If your priority is... | Consider    |
| ---------------------- | ----------- |
| Easiest setup          | Ticket Tool |
| Open-source solution   | Modmail     |
| Staff management       | Helper.gg   |
| Applications and forms | Ticketeer   |
| Large support team     | Helper.gg   |
| Appeals and reports    | Ticket Tool |

### Key Questions

**Do users contact staff through DMs?**
→ Modmail is ideal.

**Do you need application forms?**
→ Ticketeer.

**Do you manage many support agents?**
→ Helper.gg.

**Do you want the simplest setup?**
→ Ticket Tool.

## 📝 Contributor Notes

### Adding a Ticket Bot

* Ticket management must be the primary feature.
* Verify transcript support and dashboard availability.
* Confirm the bot remains actively maintained.

### Category-Specific Notes

* Many moderation bots include basic ticket systems; only include dedicated ticket solutions here.
* Recheck premium limitations periodically.
* Verify transcript retention policies when updating entries.
